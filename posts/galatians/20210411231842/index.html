Galatians 1

Posted at — Apr 11, 2021
  • Letter was written 49-50 AD 1
  • Written during the time of Emperor Claudius 2

This letter was written by the apostle Paul to the churches of Galatia. Paul describes himself as an apostle from God through Christ. He wishes the churches of Galatia grace and peace from God, and summarizes the act and will of God, to whom glory belongs to forever. v.1-5

At this point in time, the Galatian churches have been turning to a different gospel than the one preached by Paul. Anyone who preaches a different gospel is accursed. Paul reiterates that the gospel that he teaches is for the purposes of seeking the approval of God. If Paul was trying to please man, he would not be a slave of Christ. v.6-10

Purpose: To address the fact that the Galatian churches were straying away from the gospel of Jesus preached by Paul

The Gospel preached by Paul is not man’s gospel. He received it through a revelation from God. Paul proceeds to recount his conversion experience, detailing his zeal in persecuting the church and advancing Judaism. When God revealed his Son to Paul, he did not go into any of the major centres of Christianity, but to Arabia before returning to Damascus. On arriving in Jerusalem, he did not see any other apostles except Cephas and James. No one in the churches of Judea knew him in person - they only heard of it and gave glory to God because of it. v.11-24
