Galatians 5

Posted at — Apr 20, 2021

Christ set us free so we could have freedom, so do not return back to slavery. Paul, a Jew, tells them to not accept circumcision. If they do so, the will be under the law and not of grace. In Christ only faith working through love matters. Paul urges them to quickly address this matter quickly, for it only takes a few people to turn the entire group away. He urges them to use their freedom to love one another, for that is the fulfilment of the law. Even if they argue, they need to remember that.

  • Christ set them free for freedom
    • Do not submit to slavery again
  • If they accept circumcision, Christ is of no advantage to them
    • Anyone who accepts circumcision is obligated to keep the whole law
    • Severed from Christ
      • They would be justified by the law
      • Fallen away from grace
  • Those of the Spirit and faith eagerly hope for righteousness
  • In Christ circumcision or uncircumcision does not matter
    • Only faith through love
  • Galatians were doing well
  • The one who hindered the Galatians from truth is not from Christ
  • A few people can quickly affect the entire group
  • Confidence in God that the Galatians will not accept the false teaching
  • The one troubling them will suffer the penalty
  • Paul does not preach circumcision and is persecuted
  • Offence of the cross is removed if he preaches circumcision
  • Wishes those who unsettles the Galatians would emasculate themselves
  • Galatians called to freedom
  • Do not use freedom as opportunity for the flesh
    • Instead serve each other in love
  • Law is fulfilled by love
  • If they argue with each other, make sure they do not consume each other

Paul urges the Galatians to walk by the Spirit. It will be tough, since the Spirit and flesh are opposing each other, but as long as they are being led by the Spirit, they are not under the law. He gives a list of the works of the flesh, along with a warning that those who do such things will not inherit the kingdom of God. However, before the fruit of the Spirit, there is no law, as they are of Christ. He urges them again to live by and keep in step with the Spirit.

  • If you walk by the Spirit, you will not gratify the desires of the flesh
  • Desires of the flesh are against the Spirit, and vice versa
    • Keeps you from doing the things you want to do
  • If led by the Spirit, not under the law
  • Works of the flesh:
    • Sexual immorality
    • Impurity
    • Sensuality
    • Idolatry
    • Sorcery
    • Enmity
    • Strife
    • Jealousy
    • Fits of anger
    • Rivalries
    • Dissensions
    • Divisions
    • Envy
    • Drunkenness
    • Orgies
    • Anything else like the ones listed
  • Those who do the works of the flesh will not inherit the kingdom of God
  • Fruit of the spirit:
    • Love
    • Joy
    • Peace
    • Patience
    • Kindness
    • Goodness
    • Faithfulness
    • Gentleness
    • Self-control
  • Against the fruit of the Spirit there is no law
  • Christians have crucified the flesh with its passions and desires
  • Christians live by the Spirit
    • Should also keep in step with the Spirit
    • Do not become conceited
    • Do not provoke one another
    • Do not envy each other
