Day 76, Deuteronomy 17-20

Posted at — Jun 24, 2021

The laws that Moses recounts for Israel heavily emphasize judgement and grace. Many of these laws show how seriously God takes sin, as many of these offences are punishable by death or exile. The reasoning for such punishment is so that the offender becomes an example to the Israelites of what happens when they turn away from God. However, God does make a distinction between intentional and unintentional sin. In a show of grace, he gives the cities of refuge for the unintentional offender to flee to. Israel’s distinction from the surrounding nations is another recurring theme. This is shown through the laws concerning worship, the conduct of a king, and warfare. Regarding warfare, people are not forced to fight, but they are given a chance to go home and enjoy the fruits of the labour if they haven’t already. The only cities to be devoted to destruction are the ones of the specified nations - to other nations, Israel is to offer peace before war when approaching a city.
