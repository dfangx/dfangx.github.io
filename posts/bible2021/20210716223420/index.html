Day 97, 1 Samuel 1-3

Posted at — Jul 16, 2021

The birth of Samuel gives a glimpse into the hearts of the people of Israel and the priesthood of God. Hannah was barren, yet persistently prayed to God for a child. The priest Eli, although ministering in the house of God, did not see that Hannah was praying, but thought that she was drunk and likely looked down on her based on Hannah’s response. Nevertheless, God heard Hannah’s prayers and gave her Samuel. Hannah devoted Samuel to the ministry of God in return. God shows us that he listens when we pray. He is the one who provides for our needs, and Hannah responds with a prayer speaking of God’s providence, sovereignty, and curiously, judgement as well. God provides for the righteous, but judgement is given to the wicked. Eli’s sons are revealed to be evil people, and Eli does not restrain them from doing evil in the presence of God. Because of that, God promises to cut off the house of Eli, and raise in its place another priest who will be faithful. God tells Samuel this. God shows us that even if he is speaking, he will sometimes prevent us from recognizing him.
