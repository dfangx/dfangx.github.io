Day 74, Deuteronomy 11-13

Posted at — Jun 22, 2021

Moses charges Israel to love God and keep the entire commandment. He goes over the blessings that God has in store for them, but also sets a curse before them should they turn away from God and worship other gods. Moses charges Israel to set the blessing and the curse on Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal respectively. There is a heavy emphasis on worshipping God in a true manner. God is not only concerned with how Israel worships him but also where Israel worships him. This is a distinguishing factor between Israel and the surrounding nations. God will appoint a specific place where Israel can bring their offerings and sacrifices for worship. In every offering and meal, it is to be done in a manner of obedience and worship to God. In contrast, the nations at the time would use various landmarks, high places, and idols in their religious worship. Israel is charged to completely destroy such places and items, lest they are enticed to turn away from God. Anyone who entices others to turn away from God is to be put to death, so all Israel will hear and fear the consequences of turning away from God.
