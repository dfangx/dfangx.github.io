Day 34, Exodus 13-15

Posted at — May 13, 2021

The Feast of Unleavened bread was established so that the people of Israel may remember their deliverance from their Egyptian oppressors, for they left in haste. The consecration of the firstborn males was also to remember God’s deliverance, for God killed the firstborn of Egypt and redeemed his people. Thus, God led Israel out of Egypt: by day in a pillar of cloud and by night in a pillar of fire. The people are led to the Red Sea. When Pharaoh hears of it, he regrets letting Israel go and chase after them. The people are fearful, but Moses knows that God will deliver them. God urges Moses to action, and God himself becomes the barrier separating the Israelites and the Egyptians. He splits the Red Sea and the Israelites pass through safely. Only when the last Israelite had passed did the waters return, killing the Egyptians. In response to God’s deliverance, the Israelites praise God in song. Shortly after, God heals for them the bitter water of Marah, and God promises that provided they obey him, none of the diseases that were on the Egyptians will be given to them.
