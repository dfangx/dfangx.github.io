Day 106, Psalm 56, 120, 140-142

Posted at — Jul 24, 2021

Psalm 56 is an appeal from David to God to be gracious to him. He proclaims his trust and assurance in God protection. David is not afraid even when he is surrounded by enemies. His response to witnessing the grace of God is to give thanks and do what is required of him by God. Psalm 120 speaks of a threat that is hidden and subtle. Even then, God delivers the author. He cries out to God that his wish for what God wants falls on deaf ears. Psalm 140 is a cry from David for God to execute justice. He prays for protection and perseverance against those who wish to harm him, asking God to remember the cause of the afflicted. In contrast, David prays that the plans of the wicked are foiled and for God to bring judgement on them. In Psalm 140, David asks God to listen to him and act, for God knows his deeds and heart. Psalm 142 is a prayer for deliverance from David’s persecutors. He knows God will listen and guide him, even in his weakest moment.
