Day 107, 1 Samuel 25-27

Posted at — Jul 25, 2021

Samuel dies. David comes across Nabal, a Calebite. After spending some time guarding Nabal’s flock to gain trust, he sends men to request for some food as it was a feast day. However, Nabal responds with harshness and cruelty. David, upon hearing it, prepares to go to battle, but Nabal’s wife Abigail, stopped the disaster that was about to come onto Nabal’s house by offering and apology and providing David and his men what they needed. This story shows two contrasting responses to something good - one is rejection and humiliation while another returns good with good. God puts Nabal to death shortly after, and Abigail becomes David’s wife. Shortly after, Saul resumes his chase of David when he is notified of David’s location by the Ziphites. Again, God gave Saul into David’s hands and again, David refused to harm Saul. He is determined that judgement is not his to give, but God’s. David, still fearful, goes to the land of the Philistines, where he is given the city of Ziklag to live in by Achish, king of Gath. To strengthen his position, David also lies about where he went raiding, so as to gain Achish’s trust.
