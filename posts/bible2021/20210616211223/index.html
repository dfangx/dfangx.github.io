Day 68, Numbers 33-34

Posted at — Jun 16, 2021

Chapter 33 recounts the journey of Israel by dividing it into segments. The Israelites left Egypt triumphantly in the sight of the Egyptians. This is in stark contrast to the Egyptians, who were burying their first born after God’s judgement on them and their gods. Their journey takes them along the coast of the Red Sea towards the wilderness of Sin and eventually Sinai. They then turn northwards until the south side of Canaan. Because of their disobedience, they then turned back and looped through the wilderness of Zin and Paran, before travelling through the western side of Edom and Moab. They ended up on the east side of Canaan by the Jordan at Jericho. God’s command to the Israelites is to drive out all the people of Canaan, destroying all their idols and places of worship. Should they fail to do that, they will become a pain for Israel. God sets the boundaries of Israel: the Mediterranean in the west, Edom and the brook of Egypt in the south, Mount Hor in the north, and the Jordan and the Dead Sea in the east. The chiefs were put in charge of dividing the land.
