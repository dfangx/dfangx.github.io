Day 112, Psalm 6, 8-10, 14, 16, 19, 21

Posted at — Jul 30, 2021

Psalm 6 is a cry for help to God during suffering and a statement of hope in the steadfast love of God. Psalm 8 extols the majesty of God with vivid imagery of creation. The psalmist ponders on the mystery of man’s worth in relation to creation. Psalm 9 expresses thanks for God, but does so with a certain degree of emphasis on the judgement of the wicked. It expresses David’s feelings and experiences as one who has experienced remembrance and as a witness of God’s judgement on evil. Psalm 10 contrasts this by expressing how one feels when experiencing suffering, but maintains that God hears the psalmist’s cry and will bring justice. Psalm 14 summarizes the thoughts of the corrupt: there is no God. Yet, God will lift up those that the wicked oppress. Psalm 16 expresses David’s desire to hold fast to God, while pleading with God to never abandon him. Psalm 19 declares the perfection of the law of God, bringing fulfillment to those who obey it. Psalm 21 takes on the perspective of a king, praising God for not witholding anything back from him. All those blessings are centered around how the king trusts in God.
