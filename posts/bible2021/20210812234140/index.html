Day 125, Psalm 1-2, 15, 22-24, 47, 68

Posted at — Aug 12, 2021

Psalm 1 contrasts one who takes delight in the law of God and one who does not. Those who delight in the law of God are blessed and will prosper, whereas the wicked will be judged and be driven away. Psalm 2 opens with rhetorical questions asking why people rebel against God in vain. It tells us that God’s anointed will be loved by God, rule over all the earth with a rod of iron, and bless those who take refuge with him. Psalm 15 declares that those who do what is right before God will never be moved because he will be in the presence of God. Psalm 22 is an appeal for God to not abandon David. Even though David’s situation is dire, he still holds that God will deliver him so that the name of the LORD will be glorified. Psalm 23 builds on this theme by maintaining that there is nothing to fear when God is with him. Psalm 24 is a psalm of praise to God as the king of glory. Psalm 47 continues to praise God as king, but with an emphasis on the deeds of God for his people. Psalm 68 praises God for his victory over his enemies. It paints a picture of an extravagant procession and contrasts the victors and the losers of the battle.
