Day 47, Leviticus 11-13

Posted at — May 26, 2021

God gives instructions regarding what the Israelites can and cannot eat. Among the clean animals are those with a split hoof, cloven-footed, and chew the cud. Fish are also considered clean, but other sea animals like shellfish are not. Various birds of prey are also considered unclean. Insects that are clean include winged insects with jointed legs. It seems like the animals that are unclean are often associated with disease or are more difficult to prepare than clean animals. God shows the Israelites that taking care of themselves is part of their relationship with him. God’s people are to be holy because God is holy. God also gives purification rites for after childbirth. Atonement from a priest is needed, probably because childbirth and menstruation involves the shedding of blood. Various laws about leprous diseases are given. God instructs the priests on how to identify whether someone is a leper and if so, what measures to take. These measures include containment and letting others know that the individual is a leper. God’s relationship with his people dictates how each person takes care of themselves and should look for the interests of others.
