Day 136, Psalm 3-4, 12-13, 28, 55

Posted at — Aug 23, 2021

As David flees from his son Absalom, he prays to God to save him. He trusts in God to sustain him and to answer his prayer. David knows that salvation belongs to God. David sings in psalm 4 for God to answer his call. He finds his safety in God, and tells his fellow men that God has set apart the godly for himself. In psalms 12 and 13, David sings of the salvation of God from the perspective of one who feels that the faithful have all disappeared. He also sings as one who has waited very long for God to answer. Although singing in this manner, he maintains that God will move when the time is right and when he hears the groans of the poor. God will answer and turn his fortunes around. In psalm 28, God is David’s strength and shield. He keeps David safe from the work of the wicked, and lifts David up when he cries to God for help. Psalm 55 builds on the theme of relying on God for salvation. David describes his feelings when he feels burdened and troubled. However, he still trusts in God and relies on his sustenance.
