Day 14, Job 38-39

Posted at — Apr 23, 2021

God responds to Job’s complaints in a speech placing emphasis on his sovereignty and power. Although Job may not remember or perceive his presence, God is there, moving the things in the world in accordance to his will. God begins with the inanimate things in nature. He is the one created it all, and is the one has arrayed the things in nature to be as beautiful as we perceive it. God is the one who has set the mornings and nights in it’s place, and is the one who knows every detail of even the most remote or deepest places of creation. All the constellations and stars move according to what God wills. God continues to the living things of nature. He oversees the times when nature gives birth to new life. The wild things of nature listen to him. He provides each animal and creature with food to live. Where and when each creatures moves is under the command of God. The nature of each animal is determined by God. Job speaks knowing what he knows, but before God who knows all things, he cannot answer.
