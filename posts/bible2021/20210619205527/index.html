Day 71

Posted at — Jun 19, 2021

God gave King Og of Bashan over to the Israelites. Thus, Israel took control of the territory of two of the Amorite kings. This land was given to the tribes of Reuben, Gad, and Manasseh, on the condition that they continue to help the other tribes in their conquest of Canaan. Around that time, Moses pleaded with God to enter the promised land, but was denied. He was instead told to charge Joshua with the conquest of Canaan. Moses reminds Israel to obey God completely. Other nations are to see how great God is through Israel’s obedience. As part of this, Moses warns them to keep away from idolatry, reminding them of what they saw at Mount Sinai. He reminds them that God took Israel as his own inheritance, and that was independent of anything they did. Should Israel disobey, God will punish them, but if they return to God, God will have mercy on them. It is God who makes Israel the nation they are, and so they are to obey him. Moses appoints cities of refuge for the tribes beyond the Jordan, and begins to give the law to Israel.
