Day 115, 1 Chronicles 3-5

Posted at — Aug 3, 2021

Chapter 3 through 5 follow the genealogies of the tribes of Judah, Simeon, Reuban, Gad, and Manasseh. Chapter 3 also recounts the genealogy of David. Often overlooked is that even though David loved God, he also failed to keep the law, as he married the daughter of the king of Geshur - a foreigner. Again, I do not know why the small stories in this passage were included. However, I do know that of the tribes which were called out for their disobedience, all three were tribes who settled on the eastern side of the Jordan. Furthermore, although the birthright had been taken from Reuben and given to the sons of Joseph, the tribe of Manasseh did not escape exile. In this way, God shows us that status does not matter when God judges. At the same time, God also shows us that those who trust in him are not immune from falling away. The eastern tribes demonstrate this, as the author recounts that they had victory in battle when they trusted in God but was sent into exile when they turned away.
