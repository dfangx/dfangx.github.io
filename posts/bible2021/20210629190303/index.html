Day 81, Deuteronomy 32-34, Psalm 91

Posted at — Jun 29, 2021

Moses gives Israel a song and a blessing. In the song, it speaks of the righteousness and faithfulness of God. It also speaks of the greatness and sovereignty of God. The song warns Israel to stay steadfast to God and obey him, especially when times are good. Otherwise, God will turn away from them and let them experience what it is like to live without God. But God also promises to not abandon them, and there will come a time when God will redeem his people. The blessing is relatively short, but of note are the blessings of Levi and Joseph, which speak of faithfulness and prosperity respectively. The blessings begin and end with praise to God. The corresponding psalm speaks of God’s protection through any situation possible. God’s people need only to bear witness to God defeating their enemies. Nothing can stand before the people of God when they have made God their fortress. To a people who is about to lose their leader right before the beginning of the conquest, this would be a very encouraging song to hear. Moses then passes away on Mount Nebo after seeing the land, and Joshua succeeds him.
