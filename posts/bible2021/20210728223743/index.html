Day 110, Psalm 121, 123-125, 128-130

Posted at — Jul 28, 2021

These psalms are a collection of short prayers proclaiming the steadfastness of God. Similar themes run through each of them, including steadfastness, justice, and protection from evil. Structure-wise, psalms 121, 123-125, and 128 begin with a description of an action the author and God’s people have taken. It moves on to describe how God has preserved his people. Psalms 129-130 take on a slightly darker beginning by describing how God’s people have suffered under the hands of the wicked. Psalm 130 then proceeds differently than the other, in that it describes a deep yearning of the soul for God. Most of the psalms end with a call to action for the people of God. In general, God tells us that the natural response to experiencing him is to act and to yearn for him even more. Some of these psalms also describe the result of following God in comparison to the wicked for rebel against God. All of these psalms do not neglect to give praise to God and give credit to him for the protection of his people. The author never credits his protection to something he has done.
