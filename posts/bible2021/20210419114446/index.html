Day 10, Job 24-28

Posted at — Apr 19, 2021

Job describes the suffering of the poor. Although they do much work, their work is in vain. There are cries and pleas for help, but there is no charge from God. In contrast, the wicked not only do whatever they please, but their life is prolonged as well. This is also contrasted to what Job’s friends were describing about how the wicked fall. Bildad responds by reiterating that man can never be right before God. However, he seems to also dishonour God in the way he expresses this about man and creation. Job responds by giving an elaborate praise of the sovereignty, power, and majesty of God. In light of this, he continues to maintain that he has not done anything wrong. As further justification for his appeal to God, Job expresses that wisdom can only be found in the fear of God. Man is able to do so many things, but their worth pales in comparison to the worth of wisdom. Thus, Job touches on how the works of man apart from God are worthless.
