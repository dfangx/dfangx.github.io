Day 57, Numbers 7

Posted at — Jun 5, 2021

Once the tabernacle and everything in it was consecrated, the chiefs of each tribe of Israel brought their offerings. The wagons and oxen were distributed to the sons of Gershon and Merari. The sons of Kohath did not receive any because of their service to the holy things. For the dedication of the altar, each chief gave an offering of a silver plate, a silver basin, a grain offering, a burnt offering, a sin offering, and a peace offering. The value and weight of each was exactly the same for each tribe. The tribes made their offerings in order of each camp, with Judah first and Naphtali last. After the dedication, Moses went into the tent of meeting to talk with God, and God spoke to him above the mercy seat between the cherubim.
