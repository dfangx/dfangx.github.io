Day 109, 1 Samuel 28-31

Posted at — Jul 27, 2021

Israel and the Philistines go to war. Saul tries to inquire of God, but receives silence. He makes the decision to ask a medium to summon Samuel from the dead. According to the Mosaic law, mediums were not allowed in Israel. Previously, he had cut off all the mediums but now he is relying on them. When Samuel is summoned, he reiterates that all of this has come to pass because of Saul’s disobedience to God. Meanwhile, the Philistines reject David from serving as an officer in the army. God prevented David from lifting his hand against his brothers in Israel. When David and his men returned, they find Ziklag raided by the Amalekites. After inquiring of God whether he should pursue them, David pursues and defeats the Amalekites. The Philistines defeat Israel in battle, killing Saul and his sons. When the people of Jabesh Gilead hear of the treatment of the bodies of Saul and his sons, they took the bodies from the Philistines and buried them. Psalm 18 is filled with praise for God, as David thanks God for sustaining and protecting him. It paints a picture of God’s victory, with David experiencing the deliverance of God.
