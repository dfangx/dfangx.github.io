Day 103, 1 Samuel 18-20, Psalm 11, 59

Posted at — Jul 21, 2021

After David’s victory over Goliath, Jonathan and David became best friends. They loved each other very much. But Saul became jealous of David, and tried to kill him twice. Both times David evaded him. Saul grew fearful of David when he saw that God was with David but not himself. Saul then offers David his eldest daughter in marriage, but David declines. David does accept the offer to marry Saul’s younger daughter, at the price of two hundred Philistine foreskins. Saul then tries to kill David again, but Michal saves him. David heads to Ramoth and meets with Samuel. Saul chases after David, but for the second time, ends up prophesying. David brings his case to Jonathan, and they come up with a plan to determine Saul’s intent. Saul reveals his intent to kill David, and Jonathan and David part ways in tears. Psalm 11 and 59 both speak of God’s deliverance and eventual victory over his enemies. They speak of the testing of the righteous and the demise of the wicked. David finds his refuge in God - God is his strength and fortress. Thus, David can still sing praises to God even though his situation is dire.
