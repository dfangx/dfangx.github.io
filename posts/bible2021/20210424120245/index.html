Day 15, Job 40-42

Posted at — Apr 24, 2021

In the face of God’s majesty and revelation, Job finds no answer to God. God continues in his challenge to Job. Is he able to come close to what God is able to do to the proud and wicked? He describes the Behemoth, an ancient, massive beast, created so fearsome by God yet so gentle. The Leviathan also, is another one of God’s creations. Man cannot hope to tame him or kill him. Yet, it listens to his creator. The Leviathan is described to be quiet majestic and fearsome itself, a small reflection of the glory and majesty of God. Job has no answer to all of this. All he can say in the face of the challenge is that he has spoken without knowing anything about how God moves the world in accordance to his purpose. God’s purpose will always be carried through. Job, who before spoke of the absence of God and justice, now is able to see God rather than just hear about God. God tells Job’s three friends to have Job intercede for them. God accepts Job’s prayer. God blesses Job with double of what he had before. He also sends comfort and sympathy to him as well through family members. Job lives to be an old man and dies peacefully.
