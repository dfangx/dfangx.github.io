Day 130, Psalm 50, 53, 60, 75

Posted at — Aug 17, 2021

Psalm 50 expresses how God looks at the heart. God puts into perspective how the simple acts of sacrifice without having a heart after God mean nothing. Yet this is an expression of discipline and appeal for us to turn to him, as these words are spoken to the people who have a covenant with God. To those outside of God’s covenant, they are judged and rebuked. Psalm 53 tells us that there is not one who does good or seeks God. Because of this, David appeals to God to bring salvation for the people of Israel and to restore the fortunes of his people. David recognizes that the human condition can only be remedied by divine interference. Psalm 60 is interesting because it sounds like God has rejected Israel even after David returned victorious from battle. Towards the end of the psalm, it becomes apparent that David is not doing that, but giving credit to God for their victories, attributing their success to God. Psalm 75 sings of God’s righteous judgement. The wicked are doomed to fall, while the righteous are lifted up.
