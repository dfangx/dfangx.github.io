Day 127, 2 Samuel 6-7, 1 Chronicles 17

Posted at — Aug 14, 2021

David brings the ark to Jerusalem, but becomes afraid of God when God kills Uzzah for touching the ark. He sends it to the house of Obed-edom, where the ark stayed for some time. After a while, David then brings the ark into Jerusalem with a huge celebration alongside it. The people of Israel express great joy and gladness when the ark, representing God’s presence, is brought into Jerusalem. Michal, David’s wife, appears to be jealous of the way David conducted himself before the ark of God, but David appears to rebuke her for this. David then resolves to build a house for God. However, God tells David that David’s offspring will be the one to build a house for God. The offspring will be like a son of God, with God promising to discipline him and to love him. Curiously, the word “house” could be understood as a literal or a familial house. Both would make sense. In response, David accepts this and gives a prayer of thanks. In his prayer, he gives praise to God and thanks God for raising him up. He asks God to carry out the word that he has spoken.
