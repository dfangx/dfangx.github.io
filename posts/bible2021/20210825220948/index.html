Day 138, Psalm 26, 40, 58, 61, 62, 64

Posted at — Aug 25, 2021

In psalm 26, David contrasts himself with the wicked. Unlike the wicked, he has kept the law of God and walked with integrity. He will continue to do so and bless the LORD. In psalm 40, David describes his faith journey. He has experienced the salvation and deliverance of God from trouble. Therefore, he will praise God and trust in him. David knows that sacrifices and offerings do not please God by themselves - they must be accompanied with a heart after God. Having experienced God, David will not keep silent about it - rather, he will proclaim the deliverance of God to the congregation. God will continue to pour out his steadfast love and salvation on David. Psalm 58 proclaims the judgement of God onto the wicked. The righteous will find joy in it, while the wicked will perish. In psalm 61, David again proclaims that his refuge is in God. Psalm 62 builds on this, with David trusting that God will not abandon him and see that each man is paid according to his deeds. Psalm 64 also builds on the theme of salvation from the wicked. While the wicked seek to harm David, God protects him.
