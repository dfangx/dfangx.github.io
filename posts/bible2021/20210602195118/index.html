Day 54, Numbers 1-2

Posted at — Jun 2, 2021

God tells Moses to take a census of Israel. All those who are of fighting age are to be counted. The smallest tribe is Manasseh and the largest is Judah. Ephraim is listed as the main portion of Joseph in accordance to Jacob’s blessing. The tribe of Joseph also consisted of two tribes, which brings to mind Jacob’s blessing for Joseph. Although Reuben is not the largest, his tribe is still honoured as the firstborn by precedence in the census and in the camp arrangement. The camp is arranged such that the children of Rachel are in one section, the children of Bilhah and Asher of Zilpah are in another, Judah, Issachar, Zebulun are in another section, and Reuben and Simeon of Leah are numbered with Gad of Zilpah. The latter is interesting, as it recalls the blessing of Jacob. Although Reuben and Simeon are children of Leah, their actions to Jacob and the city of Schechem respectively are reflected with their numbering with one of the tribes of the concubine. The Levites are exempt from the census as they are appointed to carry and guard the tabernacle. They serve as a barrier between Israel and the wrath of God.
