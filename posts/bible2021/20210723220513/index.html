Day 105, Psalms 7, 27, 31, 34, 52

Posted at — Jul 23, 2021

These psalms speak of God’s deliverance of David from his enemies. Psalm 7 is a prayer requesting God to rise up on his behalf. David fully trusts in God to judge righteously. He asks God to not exclude him from being judged if he has done wrong, but if he as not done any wrong, then he prays that God will save him. In the midst of fleeing from Saul, David is still able to sing praises and thanksgiving to God. Psalm 27 touches on many of the same themes, but David begs God to not forsake him in a time of abandonment. His hope is in God and he will wait for God’s deliverance. Throughout Psalm 31, David elaborates further on God’s goodness and his ability to deliver David from his enemies. Psalm 34 takes on a different tone by speaking as someone who has experienced God’s goodness and deliverance. Although the righteous suffer, God hears their cries and delivers them. Finally, Psalm 52 speaks against the wicked, placing faith in God that God will tear them down.
