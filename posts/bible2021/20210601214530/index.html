Day 53, Leviticus 26-27

Posted at — Jun 1, 2021

God tells Israel the blessings he will give them if they obey him. If Israel obeys God, they will have plentiful harvests and peace, overwhelming victory over their enemies, and their land will be free from wild beasts. Furthermore, God will make them fruitful and multiply them. God will be their God and Israel his people. Language from creation and the previous covenants are used. Thus, we can confirm that all previous covenants have been inherited by the people of Israel. As part of that covenant, they will also be punished if they disobey. The punishments for continued disobedience get more and more severe, and culminates with Israel being scattered among the nations. Then the land will have the Sabbaths it missed from Israel’s disobedience. God’s decree regarding the land recalls God’s blessing to Adam, that they should rule over and work the land as God’s agents on earth. However, God also gives an opportunity for repentance should Israel turn away. Although the land will still have its rest, God will remember his scattered people. Laws regarding vows are also given, each in relation to the year of Jubilee. Additionally, tithes belong to God, so they cannot be dedicated.
