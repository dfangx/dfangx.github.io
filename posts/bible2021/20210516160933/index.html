Day 37. Exodus 22-24

Posted at — May 16, 2021

Laws regarding repayment of damages are given. These laws show that God ensures that those who are wronged are repaid, and those who do wrong pay the full amount. God sits as judge and arbitrator over those disputes. Regarding social justice, these laws cover topics ranging from giving to the poor, adultery, and more. It shows God’s compassion for the poor and desire for justice. Laws regarding the Sabbath and other festivals are also given, which serve as a reminder for not only God’s deliverance of Israel from the Egyptians, but also as a reminder that God is the one sustains them. God promises to send an angel before Israel as they advance into Canaan. Provided Israel obeys him, God will drive out the people of the land in a way that allows Israel to completely possess the land. The covenant is confirmed with sacrifices and blood. It brings to mind the earlier covenant confirmation between God and Abraham. Then, contrary to the previous fear of seeing God, God invites Moses and the elders up to the mountain, where they feasted and drank.
