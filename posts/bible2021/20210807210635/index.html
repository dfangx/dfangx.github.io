Day 120, Psalm 102-104

Posted at — Aug 7, 2021

Psalm 102 expresses the heart of someone afflicted and experiencing suffering. It is during these times when the author feels like God is punishing him for something. However, the author also holds that even if that is the case, God will remember his servants and lift them up. When that happens, the nations will fear God and the people will gather to worship God. God will never fade and thus, his children will be able to dwell securely in God’s presence eternally. Psalm 103 is a psalm of praise. God reveals more of who he is to us through this psalm. He is righteous, just, merciful, gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love. The author borrows the powerful words found in Exodus, invoking the memory of when God revealed himself to Moses and to Israel. It ends with a calling for all those who serve God to bless the LORD. Psalm 104 continues with this theme, but utilizes heavy imagery found in creation. This psalm emphasizes God as the creator, and reminds us that all the beauty in the world was created by God. The author desires to worship in the presence of God, while the wicked are no more.
