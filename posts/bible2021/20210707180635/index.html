Day 89,

Posted at — Jul 7, 2021

After Joshua died, Israel continued their conquest of Canaan. However, there eventually came a time when Israel stopped driving out the Canaanites. Although the Canaanites were put into forced labour, the failure to conquer the land was in disobedience to God. Thus, God stopped driving out the Canaanites before them, instead letting them be a snare to Israel. Parts of this passage bear similarity to the wording found in Exodus - specifically the description of Joshua’s death, the growing up of another generation, and Israel’s defeat by their enemies can be compared to Joseph’s death, the seating of a new Pharaoh who did not know Joseph, and Egypt’s oppression of Israel. Similarly, when God heard their cry in Egypt, God also heard their cry when they were oppressed. Like in Egypt, he sent people (judges) to save them. However, Israel’s disobedience has now resulted in the curses becoming true, and the nations not driven out were now used to test Israel to see if they would be faithful.
