Day 82, Joshua 1-4

Posted at — Jun 30, 2021

Joshua officially takes over leadership of Israel. God reiterates what he said before to Joshua, but this time in his new capacity as leader. Joshua orders the people of Israel to prepare for the crossing and reminds the Reubenites, Gadites, and Manassites of their oath to help the Israelites in the conquest. He also sends spies into the land. The spies enter the city of Jericho and are hidden from the king of Jericho by a prostitute named Rahab. Rahab reveals that the people of the land had heard of the great things God had done, and that everyone feared God and Israel. By this she knew that Israel would be victorious. Thus, she made the spies swear an oath to spare her and her family. The sign of salvation for them would be a scarlet cord tied to the window. Joshua and Israel then cross the Jordan. When the priests carrying the ark entered the overflowing Jordan, the waters parted. This brings to mind the event of the Red Sea crossing. By this, God exalted Joshua, just as he had done with Moses. Twelve stones were set up as a memorial of this event.
