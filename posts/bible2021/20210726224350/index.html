Day 108, Psalm 17, 35, 54, 63

Posted at — Jul 26, 2021

In psalm 17, David pleas his righteousness before God. He compares himself to the wicked who speak and act against God. He pleads with God to take action against his enemies, so that the righteous may find satisfaction in him. Psalm 35 continues the theme of pleading for God’s justice to be shown. The psalm describes David as surrounded by many enemies, and although he had wished peace and wellbeing for them, they return his actions with evil. Thus, David pleads with God to rise up and act so that they may know the LORD is God. David’s desires the justice of God not for his own sake, but so that God may be glorified and be given the praise that he deserves. Psalm 54 sings of the steadfastness of God. Although continuously pursued and betrayed, God has delivered David every time, so even now, he looks to God for his deliverance. Psalm 64 asks God to hide David from the wicked. The wicked are out to harm him through devious and direct ways. However, David sings of God striking them when they least expect it, with the fear of God coming on those who hear of it.
