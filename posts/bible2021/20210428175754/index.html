Day 19, Genesis 22-24

Posted at — Apr 28, 2021

When God tests Abraham, Abraham readily obeys, willing to give up the manifestation of God’s promise in obedience to God. Curiously, Abraham tells his son Isaac that God will provide the lamb, although God has not said anything regarding that. Abraham’s faith in God is attested to when God indeed provides a ram for him to offer as an offering instead of his son. Soon afterwards, Sarah dies, and is buried in a cave purchased by Abraham from Ephron the Hittite. The story of Isaac and Rebekah is another show of God’s providence not only for Abraham, but all those in his household. Abraham sends his servant to his homeland to find a wife for Isaac with few instructions. His servant prays to God and asks for a clear and discernible sign, which God answers in the form of Rebekah. From the testimony of the servant, Rebekah’s family also recognizes that this was from God. In seeing God’s providence, the servant urgently and eagerly completes the mission he was given - he requests that Rebekah go with him immediately to Isaac. Not only does this provide Isaac a wife but it also provides him with comfort from his mother’s death.
