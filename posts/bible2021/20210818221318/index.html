Day 131, 2 Samuel 10, 1 Chronicles 19, Psalm 20

Posted at — Aug 18, 2021

Nahash, king of the Ammonites died. David sent men to comfort his son Hanun as a gesture of friendship twowards the late king. However, on the advice of the young princes, Hanun shamed them and sent them away. When the Ammonites saw that David had become their enemy, they brought together an alliance of nations to fight against Israel. God gave Israel victory over the alliance. However, when the Syrians saw they were defeated, they regrouped and brought in an even larger alliance. Once again, God brought victory to Israel. Psalm 20 builds on the theme of trusting in God’s salvation. The psalm exclaims that to trust in God means victory is assured, while trusting in weapons of war means certain ruin. God is the one who protects and sends help. Even the king is saved by God.
