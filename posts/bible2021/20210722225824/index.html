Day 104, 1 Samuel 21-24

Posted at — Jul 22, 2021

David flees to Nob, where he requests bread for the priests. The only bread present was the bread of the Presence - only the priests were permitted to eat it. David lies to Ahimelech the priest, and Ahimelech gives the bread to David, disregarding the law so that David would have food. David continues on to Gath and eventually to Moab. Saul, on hearing from an Edomite that Ahimelech assisted David, condemns the priests at Nob and kills them. Saul shows his disregard for God, elevating himself above God. David saves the city of Keilah from the Philistines after asking for God’s guidance, and leaves Keilah when he learns from God that Keilah will hand him over to Saul. God saves David from the nick of being captured in the wilderness by sending a report to Saul that the Philistines were raiding Israel. God also places Saul into David’s hands some time later, but David refused to lift his hand against God’s anointed even though his men urged David to kill Saul. David knows that he is in no place to judge Saul. As Saul was God’s anointed, judgement of Saul belonged to God.
