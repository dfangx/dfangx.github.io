Day 102, 1 Samuel 15-17

Posted at — Jul 20, 2021

God gives Saul the command to completely destroy the Amalekites for how they treated Israel when Israel came out of Egypt. Saul defeats the Amalekites but does not devote all of them to destruction, leading to disobedience to God. As a result, God rejects Saul as king over Israel. God shows us that sacrifices mean nothing to God when it isn’t done in obedience. Saul tries to defend himself and his honour, showing how prideful he had become. Although Samuel goes with Saul at the end, that would be the last time he sees Saul. God then sends Samuel to anoint David as king. David’s description is in contrast with Saul’s description: Saul’s description during his anointment described his stature and physical traits while David was the opposite. God reveals to us that he looks at the heart, not their physical appearance. God was with David as he served Saul as his musician, and with him when he defeated Goliath. The defeat of Goliath was symbolic of God defeating the gods of the Philistines, as Goliath had been slandering the God of Israel. In this way, God delivered Israel from the Philistines.
