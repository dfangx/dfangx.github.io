Day 40, Exodus 30-32

Posted at — May 19, 2021

Laws for the altar of incense are given. Only Aaron is able to burn incense on it. No unauthorized incense or offerings are allowed on the altar. Once a year, atonement is made on its horns. A census tax is instituted, which serves as a ransom for the life of anyone named in the census. A bronze basin is made for the priests to wash in. Finally God instructs them to make an anointing oil. Anything it touches becomes holy to God. Any replication or misuse results in the offender being cut off from Israel. Through his Spirit, Oholiab and Bezalel are given the ability to make everything. The Sabbath is officially instituted as part of the covenant. God wrote all this on two stone tablets. However, Aaron leads Israel to sin with the golden calf. Although God is very angry, he relents from destroying the people at Moses' intercession. Upon seeing the people and the calf, Moses breaks the tablets in anger, and God renders judgement on Israel through the Levites and a plague. The first punishment from God comes after the covenant has been made. Moses returns to the mountain to intercede for the people, and God responds with an emphasis on his righteousness.
