Day 88, Joshua 22-24

Posted at — Jul 6, 2021

With the land subdued and portioned, Joshua sends the eastern tribes of Reuben, Gad, and Manasseh back home beyond the Jordan. They had fulfilled their promise in helping the rest of Israel subdue the land, and they were given a reminder to obey everything that God had commanded them. The eastern tribes decided to build an altar by the boundary at the Jordan. This angered the rest of Israel, who saw it as a breach in faith, bringing back reminders of the punishments that they suffered in the wilderness. The eastern tribes explained that they set it up as a witness, should the other tribes decide to cut off the eastern tribes from serving God. Whether this was genuine or not is hard to tell, but it is notable that they did not seek the counsel of God in this incident. Some time later, Joshua reminds Israel to keep the charge of God and of all the good things that God has done. He warns them against turning away from God. God renews the covenant with Israel a third time, ending with Israel agreeing to serve God. Joshua dies and is buried in his inheritance.
