Day 45, Leviticus 5-7

Posted at — May 24, 2021

God also takes into account one’s ability to give the sin offering when he gives the laws. Among the offences that require a sin offering include uncleanness, failure to testify, or any sort of rash oath. In those cases a sin offering is brought to the priest and the priest makes atonement for the individual. There are also laws regarding guilt offerings, which the priest also makes atonement for. God then gives instructions to the priests. Regarding the burnt offering, the priests are to keep the fire on the altar burning continually. When interacting with things that are holy, the priest must be wearing the garments that are consecrated. For certain offerings like the grain offering, the priest is allowed to eat a portion of it. Thus, in partaking in the ministry of God, God also sustains the priests by providing what the priests need in the structure and form of worship that is used. The offerings are holy to God. Anyone who interacts with the offering in an improper manner sins against God.
