Day 119, 1 Chronicles 7-10

Posted at — Aug 6, 2021

The author recounts the genealogies of the tribes of Issachar, Benjamin, Naphtali, Manasseh, Ephraim, and Asher. Each genealogy is of varying lengths with varying degrees of detail, likely reflecting the sources that were accessible to the author. The author also chooses to recount the genealogy of Saul, followed by the genealogy of the returned exiles. In doing so, the author establishes the identity of the origin and history of Israel and relates it to the identity of the returning exiles. The author seems to recognize the importance of Saul’s genealogy as it is repeated twice. Alternatively, the second genealogy of Saul may simply be a way for the author to transition into the story of the death of Saul. The recount of the death of Saul is very similar to the narrative told in 1 Samuel. However, the author emphasizes that the fall of Saul came because he did not keep the commandment of God. To the returning exiles, this would be a reminder to them to keep the law of God, lest they fall as well.
