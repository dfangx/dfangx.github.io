Day 64, Numbers 23-25

Posted at — Jun 12, 2021

Balaam gave a total of four oracles concerning Israel. Balaam went to look for omens from God for the first two, while for the final two, he was aware that God intended to bless Israel and did not go to look for omens from God. The one who seeks out God receives what he seeks, while the one who knows the will of God and does it receives the Spirit of God. Balaam’s oracles contain blessings of fruitfulness, victory, and perseverance. It extols the sovereignty of God as what God has said will be fulfilled and not changed. They bring a message of God’s salvation and providence for Israel. There is even a prophecy about a being who will crush the enemies of Israel, and the downfall of the surrounding nations. These oracles and actions of Balaam are immediately contrasted with those of Israel, who in the meantime, have begun to whore with the daughters of Moab. The consequence was a plague from God, killing many of them, and was stopped with the zeal of Phinehas for God. Thus, Phinehas and his descendants are given a covenant of perpetual priesthood and of peace, and Midian becomes an enemy to Israel.
