Day 95, Judges 19-21

Posted at — Jul 14, 2021

The final narrative in Judges begins the same way as the previous, emphasizing that there was no king in Israel back then. The author alerts us to the neglect of the law of God when Gibeah does not show hospitality to the sojourning Levite. When the men of Gibeah went up against the Levite at the place he was staying, it bore a striking similarity to the story of Sodom and Gommorah. One must wonder why Gibeah did not suffer the same fate as Sodom and Gommorah. One must also ask why the Levite decided to chop up his dead concubine and send it throughout the tribes of Israel. As a consequence for all this, Israel goes into civil war. God shows us that when sin is left unattended, the effects of it have farther reach, accompanied with more extreme actions. The final battle against Gibeah bears a striking resemblance to the battle against Ai. It is also disturbing as to how Israel can use God’s name when deciding how to obtain wives for Benjamin. The book of Judges ends with a recurring theme: There was no king in Israel. Everyone did what was right in his own eyes.
