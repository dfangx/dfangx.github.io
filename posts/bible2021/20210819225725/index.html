Day 132, Psalm 65-67, 69-70

Posted at — Aug 19, 2021

Psalms 65-67 share the common theme of praising God for his goodness and salvation. The strength of God is often depicted using the forces of nature, while the holiness and justice of God are expressed with either praise of God’s salvation or an appeal to God to execute judgement on the wicked. The psalms remind us that it is God who provides for both nature and man. Furthermore, God’s love is often expressed not only through blessing but also through discipline. The works of God are always responded to by the author with action. Witnessing and experiencing God in such a way naturally leads to the singing of praises and keeping of the law. The author understands that if he loved wickedness, God would not be listening to his prayers and psalms. In psalm 69, David goes to great lengths to describe his feelings as one being persecuted for obeying God. He does not stop living for God but rather increases his zeal for God. He appeals to God to see his suffering and make judgement on his enemies. David does not hesitate to praise God. Psalm 70 builds o this by asking God to not delay in taking action.
