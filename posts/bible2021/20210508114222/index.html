Day 29, Genesis 48-50

Posted at — May 8, 2021

The progression of the final story is different, as one would expect for God to appear to the next inheritor of the Abrahamic covenant. This does not happen, which tells us something big is about to happen. Ephraim and Manasseh are to be counted as sons of Jacob and receives his blessing, with the younger gaining preeminence over the older. Jacob then blesses his sons. Reuben, although firstborn, does not get preeminence as the firstborn, as he defiled his father’s bed. Simeon and Levi get a blessing according to their actions against Schechem. Curiously, Judah gets a blessing of power and authority. The sons of the maids each get shorter blessings, but Dan’s stands out as one who will retain authority of judgement even though he is an inferior tribe. Joseph will suffer, but he will be fruitful by the hand of God. Benjamin’s blessing contains language of war. Jacob passes away and in obedience to their father, his sons bury him with his fathers. Genesis concludes with two main points: God can use the evil of mankind for his greater good purpose, and God will bring the people of Israel out of Egypt and back to Canaan.
