Day 58, Numbers 8-10

Posted at — Jun 6, 2021

God orders Moses to cleanse the Levites by sprinkling the water of purification and giving a grain and sin offering. The Levites are offered as a wave offering to God before the entire congregation. In this way, the Levites are separated from the rest of Israel, for they are taken in the place of the firstborn of Israel. On retirement from their ministry, the Levites are required to keep guard over the tabernacle. At this time, the Passover is celebrated. It is of such importance that even those who are unclean are permitted to take part. God also commands Moses to make two silver trumpets, which serve as signals to move camp or gather and to call on God before battle. In addition, Israel lifted camp whenever the cloud lifted and keeping camp otherwise. At this point in time, Israel left Sinai. The first camp left first, followed by the sons of Gershon and Merari. Then followed the second camp and the Kohathites. The final two camps brought up the rear. Moses' father in law was also invited to partake of the future blessings of God and to act as a guide.
