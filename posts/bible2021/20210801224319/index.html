Day 114, Psalm 43-45, 49, 84-85, 87

Posted at — Aug 1, 2021

Psalm 43 appeals to God to lift up the author and to defend him against the wicked. He questions why he is oppressed even though he is faithful. He calls on God to act, so he may praise God. Psalm 44 continues in the theme of asking where God is in the midst of oppression and suffering. It describes the feeling of how one feels when they suffer even though they are faithful to God. The psalm ends with another appeal for God to rise up and redeem his people. Psalm 45 appears to use human imagery to describe the reign of God, and paints a glorious picture of what it looks like. Psalm 49 addresses why one should not fear in times of trouble. Man can build up wealth but in the end it amounts to nothing, while only God has the power to ransom the life of man. Psalm 84 expresses a longing to dwell in the presence of God - a single day with God is better than a thousand anywhere else. Psalm 85 expresses a desire for God to revive his people. He has shown them favour, and revival comes when God gives righteousness and faithfulness. Psalm 87 speaks of a glory that is to come, when Zion is known throughout the world for being the dwelling place of God.
