Day 101, 1 Samuel 13-14

Posted at — Jul 19, 2021

Saul goes to war against the Philistines. However, increasing defections begin to make Saul very nervous. As a result, instead of waiting for Samuel to come, he offers the sacrifice unlawfully. When Samuel arrives later that day, Saul tries to defend himself, but Samuel still tells him that his kingdom will no longer continue. God shows us that placing trust in him involves not just what is done but how it is done. The consequences for the king are heavy, as the king is supposed to be God’s agent for his people. Following this, God uses Saul’s son, Jonathan, to save Israel. Saul shows his character as someone who is beginning to rely on himself rather than God when he decides to pursue the Philistines rather than consult God first. Following this, Saul makes a rash vow. Considering the actions so far, he might have been jealous of Jonathan. Jonathan breaks the vow, but the men ransom Jonathan from the consequence of the vow, saving him.
