Day 116, Psalm 73, 77-78

Posted at — Aug 3, 2021

Psalm 73 seeks to explore and understand why the wicked prosper while the righteous suffer. It conveys the feelings of the heart when it says that it is envious of the prosperity of the wicked. They mock God in what they say and do. As the author seeks to understand, he finds it wearisome until he goes into the presence of God. There, he finds comfort and strength to continue to trust in God and listen to God’s guidance. Psalm 77 conveys the feelings during times when worshipping God is painful. In those times, the author questions whether God cares or whether he is faithful. The author resolves to remember the deeds of God, especially when he redeemed his people. The psalm ends by remembering the power of God over creation and his leadership of his people. Psalm 78 serves as a reminder of the deeds of God to the coming generations. However, what is recounted here are the deeds of God when Israel sinned. This serves as a warning for future generations. The latter half is a message of hope. Although Israel turned back to God half-heartedly, God atoned for their sins and saved them.
