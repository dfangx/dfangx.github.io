Day 123, Psalm 106-107

Posted at — Aug 10, 2021

Psalm 106 is about the goodness of God. At first glance, it seems that this is simply a psalm of thanksgiving. However, we quickly realize that this is a psalm that thanks God for his goodness in spite of the repeated offences of his people. The psalmist recounts every time Israel sinned against God. It puts into perspective that God could have destroyed Israel at any time. However, out of God’s goodness, whether it be by his patience and grace or by the intervention of someone chosen by God, he spared them. The author appeals to God’s goodness to save them so that they may give thanks and glory to God. Psalm 107 continues in the theme of thanksgiving. There are three groups of people described: one that encounters trouble in life, one in active intentional rebellion against God, and one that is indifferent and goes about his own business. The first group comes to God when they experience God’s salvation. The second group comes to God when they experience God’s discipline. The third group comes to God when they bear witness to the power of the Creator. Each method comes to be because of the steadfast love of God.
