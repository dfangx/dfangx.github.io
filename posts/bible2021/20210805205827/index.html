Day 118, Psalm 81, 88, 92-93

Posted at — Aug 5, 2021

Psalm 81 appears to switch between the perspective of the author and the perspective of God. Yet, it is apparent that the heart of both desires that God’s people listen and obey God. It is a prayer that expresses God’s desire to bless and lift up his people while protecting them through the judgement of the wicked. Psalm 88 expresses the feelings of someone crying out to God. Although he is afflicted and oppressed, the author still determines that the worst thing that can happen for him is to go through all of that apart from God. The author does not hold back in his feelings, singing how he feels that all of his misfortune is because God had departed from him. In contrast, psalm 92 is a psalm of thanksgiving. It almost feels like a response to psalm 88, as the psalmist praises God for who he is and what he has done and is doing. It conveys that man could never comprehend the thoughts of God. Psalm 93 continues in praising God but with more of an emphasis on the greatness of God. It depicts God as a king, one who rules with trust and holiness eternally.
