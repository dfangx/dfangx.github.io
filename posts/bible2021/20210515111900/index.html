Day 36, Exodus 19-21

Posted at — May 15, 2021

The Israelites arrive at Mount Sinai. There, God begins to establish the Mosaic covenant. As the offspring of Abraham, Israel inherits the Abrahamic covenant as a people and get a new covenant, one that would set apart Israel as God’s treasured possession. God warns the people to consecrate themselves and to stay away from the mountain, for God himself was about to meet with the people of Israel. God descends on the mountain in smoke, fire, and thunder. The mountain trembled, and his descent was heralded by trumpets. No one was allowed to go up to the mountain except Moses and Aaron, whom God permitted. The ten commandments were given, with the first four detailing one’s conduct before God and the last six detailing conduct towards others. Laws dictating altars for sacrifices to God were also given. In addition, laws detailing Israel’s day to day life, in particular, regarding slaves and property, were given. Thus, after God presented them with salvation from the Egyptians, he now begins to detail what sort of relationship he would have with Israel and vice versa.
