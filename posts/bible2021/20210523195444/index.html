Day 44, Leviticus 1-4

Posted at — May 23, 2021

The laws concerning burnt offerings, peace offerings, and sin offerings all use some sort of animal as the offering. All sacrifices and offerings take place just outside the tent of meeting. A portion of the grain offering may also be given to the priests for food, and it is that portion which God sets apart as most holy. In the case of the burnt, peace, and sin offerings, the killed animal’s blood plays an important role, as it is sprinkled onto the horns and poured onto the sides of the altar of burnt offering. The sin offering for unintentional sin of an individual also utilizes the altar of incense inside the tent of meeting. God also distinguishes between unintentional sin of the entire congregation, of a leader, or of an individual. For those offerings, blood is sprinkled before the veil in the tent of meeting and poured out at the base of the altar of burnt offering. The priest makes atonement for the offender(s).
