Day 126, Psalm 89, 96, 100-101, 132

Posted at — Aug 13, 2021

Psalm 89 sings of the steadfast love of God. The primary way which it expresses this is through the covenant between David and God. It puts into perspective how powerful God is over all creation and despite having that power, still choosing to form a covenant with David. It is somewhat confusing, as the psalmist sings that God will bless his anointed but also about the affliction of the anointed. However, the psalmist maintains that God will not remove them from his love, even when the offspring of David rebel against him. Psalm 96 declares the glory of God through his righteousness and sovereignty. All the earth worship God because he is coming in judgement, which is a strange thing to say. Psalm 100 also sings of God’s love for us and emphasizes the relationship between man and God as creation and creator. Psalm 101 is interesting because although it sings of the steadfast love of God, it expresses this by stating that God will destroy all the things that God hates, which are incompatible with God’s love. Psalm 105 remembers all the deeds of God done for Israel, and is a reminder for us of what God has done in our lives and give thanks to God for them. Psalm 132 is an appeal to God to remember that God has chosen Zion as the his place for worship. The psalmist asks God to do this for the sake of his everlasting covenant with David.
