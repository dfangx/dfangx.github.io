Day 4, Job 1-5

Posted at — Apr 13, 2021

Job was one of the greatest men in the east. He was blameless and righteous before before God and feared God. One day, Satan was in the presence of God, and at the request of Satan, God permitted him to bring disaster on Job’s property. Job lost everything, but maintained his integrity by refusing to curse God, and instead, praised him. Satan and God converse again, and God permits Satan to bring harm to Job’s health. However, even when his wife told him to curse God and die, Job refuses and acknowledges that what God has the right to give also has the right to take. When Job’s friends come to see him, they are grieved. Afterwards, Job curses the day of his birth. Eliphaz answers by maintaining the holiness of God and that those who are innocent do not perish. He continues maintaining that man can never be right or pure before God, and that he would seek God for justice. Those who are disciplined by God are blessed, and those who he brings misfortune to he also saves and heals. Someone who goes through God’s discipline has nothing to fear and will prosper.
