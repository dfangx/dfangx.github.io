Day 9, Job 21-23

Posted at — Apr 18, 2021

While Job’s friends continue to insist that the wicked are brought to ruin, Job counters by describing the prosperity of the wicked. The wicked are described as those who do not fear God and do not want to listen to his ways. Eliphaz, in response, accuses Job of many iniquities, most of which are probably false. However, there is much truth about God spoken, in that even the most righteous of men would not bring any profit to God. He insists that Job must repent before God can lift him back up. Job maintains that he does not see the justice of God in his life. Not only does he see the wicked prosper, but God does not answer him when he calls. He is searching for God but does not find him. Still, in faith, he still believes that God is there and watching him, and desires to bring his complaints before God. Even though many misfortunes are upon him, he will not be silent or afraid to speak to God.
