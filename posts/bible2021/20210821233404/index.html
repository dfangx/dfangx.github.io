Day 134, Psalm 32, 51, 86, 122

Posted at — Aug 21, 2021

Psalm 32 speaks of the those who have been forgiven by God. To be forgiven by God means that their sin has been covered up by God; however, to do this, they must uncover their sin before God. Those who have been forgiven by God are under God’s protection - they will enjoy the steadfast love of God. In psalm 51, David builds on the theme of God covering up the sin of his people but focuses more on the aftermath. After God covers up the sin of his people, he begins to create a new heart in the individual - one that hates sin and wants to live in accordance to who God is. It is a heart that desires others to know the way of salvation. Psalm 86 sings of the steadfast love of God, focusing on the joy and thanksgiving that comes with experiencing his love. Psalm 122 is a prayer of David for all the tribes of Israel to go up to the house of God. It expresses David’s joy in going there, and is also a prayer for peace in the city of God for the sake of the house of God.
