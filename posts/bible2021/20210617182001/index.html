Day 69, Numbers 35-36

Posted at — Jun 17, 2021

A total of forty-eight cities and the surrounding pasturelands are to be given to the Levites to live in. Of those, six of them are cities of refuge. The cities of refuge offer protection to someone who has killed someone without intent. There, the avenger of blood, or the one who seeks judgement, cannot harm the killer without trial - it is a sanctuary for the killer. However, if the person has committed murder, then the murderer is to be put to death - the cities of refuge offer no protection to a murderer. So not only does the congregation have the responsibility to see that atonement is made for the shed blood, they also have the responsibility to see that someone who is in the protection of the city of refuge does not die until he is found guilty. The actions of Israel also reflect on their relationship with the land, for God’s people live in the land, and God dwells among the people. In addition, God brings further clarity to the laws surrounding female heirs. To ensure that no clan or tribe loses a portion of their inheritance, female heirs must marry someone within their clan.
