Day 59, Numbers 11-13

Posted at — Jun 7, 2021

After forming the covenant and leaving Sinai, the people begin complaining about their misfortunes. Fire comes down and consumes parts of the camp, and Moses prays on behalf of the people for the fire to stop. Later, the people complain about not having meat, going as far as misremembering their treatment in Egypt. At this point, the burden on Moses became too heavy, and he brought his own complaints before God. In response, God directs Moses to appoint elders over the people. God’s Spirit is placed on them and they begin to prophesy, but only for a little while. Others not there also temporarily received the Spirit, and Moses remarks that he wished all of God’s people would receive the Spirit. The cravings of the people were satisfied when God sent quail - however, they also received a plague as punishment for their complaints. Aaron and Miriam oppose Moses soon after and in response, God describes the relationship he has with Moses. Miriam was afflicted with leprosy and was barred form the camp for the required time. Afterwards, twelve spies were sent to spy out the land of Canaan. Only Caleb of Judah brought a positive report.
