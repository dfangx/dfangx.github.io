Day 30, Exodus 1-3

Posted at — May 9, 2021

The children of Israel increased in number and strength. In time, the Egyptians began to fear the Israelites and oppressed them, making them slaves. Pharaoh ordered the Hebrew midwives to kill all male Hebrew newborns. However, the midwives feared God and disobeyed Pharaoh, so God blessed them. Pharaoh orders all Hebrew newborns to be cast into the Nile. In time, a baby is born to a family in the house of Levi. After hiding him for 3 months, the baby was put into a basket and sent down the Nile where an Egyptian princess found him. He was named Moses, and grew up as an Egyptian prince while being raised a Hebrew. After some years, he sees an Egyptian beating a Hebrew. Moses intervenes and kills the Egyptian. Another dispute occurs between two Hebrews; this results in his flight to Midian. In Midian, he marries Zipporah, the daughter of a Midianite priest. They have a son name Gershom. God hears the cry for rescue from Israel. Thus, God visits Moses via a burning bush, and commands him to lead Israel out of Egypt. It is here that God first makes his name known, and God also gives the words to say.
