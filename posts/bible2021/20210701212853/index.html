Day 83, Joshua 5-8

Posted at — Jul 1, 2021

The people west of the Jordan heard of Israel’s Jordan crossing by God’s hand, and they all lost heart. Remarkably, this was the time when God told Joshua to circumcise the new generation of Israel, since they would be putting themselves in a very vulnerable state in the middle of enemy territory. Joshua’s encounter with the commander of the army of God brings to mind Moses’s encounter with God at the burning bush. The fall of Jericho and the defeat and victory with the people of Ai demonstrate the relationship that God and Israel were to have. God was with them provided that they held faith in obeying God, as shown in Jericho and in the eventual victory at Ai. However, the sin of an individual could reflect corporately on Israel, as displayed with Achan’s sin. This resulted in the defeat of Israel by Ai. After those battles, Joshua renewed the covenant by obeying all that Moses commanded him to do regarding the law in the land of Canaan.
