Titus 1

Posted at — Jun 17, 2021
  • Setting of writing:
    • Between 60 AD and 66 AD 1
      • During the reign of Nero 2
      • Possibly written in between imprisonments in Rome 3
    • Written from Nicopolis in Epirus 1

This letter is written by Paul, a slave of God and an apostle of Christ, for Titus, his child in faith. v.1-4

  • Author: Paul

    • Who he is:
      • Servant of God v.1
        • Q: What does it mean to be a servant of God?
          • Greek word doulos which means slave
            • In Rome:
              • Lowest class, less rights than freed criminals 4
              • Considered property 4
              • Sometimes stole, worked more slowly, sabotaged, as protests against treatment 4
          • Interpretations:
            • One who completely belongs to God in all aspects of life
            • One who lives for the interests of God
            • One who has no rights as an individual
      • Apostle of Jesus Christ v.1
        • Q: What is an apostle?
          • Usually used with respect to the Twelve and Paul
          • Requirement according to Acts 1:21-26:
            • One who walked with Jesus from his baptism to his ascension
            • Includes hearing the teachings of Jesus, witnessing the miracles performed, witnessing his death, resurrection, and ascension
          • Jesus is considered an apostle Hebrews 3:1
          • One who is sent forth (Dictionary definition) Luke 11:49
          • Chosen by Jesus Mark 3:14, Luke 6:13, Acts 1:2, 1 Corinthians 12:28, etc
          • Q: How can Paul be an apostle?
            • Jesus revealed himself to Paul (witness) Acts 9:1-19, 1 Corinthians 15:8, Galatians 1:16
            • Jesus chose Paul to preach the Gospel to the Gentiles (chosen and sent)
            • Paul considers himself as one of the apostles 1 Corinthians 15:9, Galatians 1:17
            • His status and teaching is approved by the other apostles Galatians 2:7-9
        • Indicates a level of authority
    • The reason he is who he is:
      • For the sake of:
        • Faith of God’s elect v.1
          • God’s elect: the Church
        • Knowledge of truth v.1
    • Both reasons:
      • Agrees with godliness v.1
      • Are in hope of eternal life v.2
        • Promised by God v.2
          • God never lies v.2
          • Before the ages began v.2
          • Q: How was it promised by God?
            • Man’s design was to live eternally Genesis 2
            • All the Scripture that points to Christ, whether it be by prophecy or by foreshadowing
            • A prophecy about the resurrection of the dead Isaiah 26:19
            • Jews definitely lived in expectation of receiving eternal life Luke 18:18
        • Manifested in his word at the proper time v.3
          • Q: How was eternal life manifested in his word?
            • Through the person of Jesus Christ John
          • Paul entrusted with it v.3
            • By command of God our Saviour v.3
            • Relates back to his apostleship v.1
        • There is an element of God’s sovereignty in his description
    • Q: Why is it important that Paul introduces himself this way?
      • The things Paul preaches is from God
      • How Paul lives is in a manner of total obedience and submission to God
      • God is the one in charge, not Paul

  • Recipient: Titus v.4

    • Paul’s true child in common faith v.4
    • Brought with Paul to the Jerusalem Council Galatians 2:1-3, Acts 15
    • Was Greek Galatians 2:3
    • Mentioned twelve times in Paul’s letters
    • Described as a “partner and fellow worker” 2 Corinthians 8:23
    • Has brought much joy and comfort to Paul 2 Corinthians 7:6
    • Q: Why does Paul identify Titus like this?
      • Led to faith by Paul
      • Describes a father-son relationship

Paul left Titus in Crete to shepherd the Christian community there and to appoint elders. An elder is to live a godly life within his family and in relationship with others. Furthermore, they must be rooted in sound doctrine, teaching others about it and rebuking those who contradict it. v.5-9

  • Titus:
    • Left in Crete by Paul
      • Island in the Mediterranean, between Asian Minor and Greece
      • History of piracy in the Mediterranean 5
      • Conquered by Rome in 63BC 5
      • Largely uneventful 5
      • Joint province with Cyrenaica in North Africa 5
      • Prosperous island
        • Oil and wine production 6
        • Roman culture expanded after its conquest 6
        • Minted own coinage 6
    • Why:
      • Put whatever remained in order
        • Q: What does this mean?
          • Part of Paul’s fourth missionary journey, so the church there would have been very young 3
          • Unfinished work
      • Appoint elders in every town as directed by Paul v.5
        • Q: What are elders and their role in the church?
          • In Israel:
            • Leaders in the tribes of Israel Exodus 4:11
            • Representatives of the clans and tribes Exodus 12, 17
            • Officers in Israel Numbers 11:16
            • Assisted Moses in carrying the burden of the people Numbers 11:16-17
            • Oversaw justice Deuteronomy 19:11-12, Deuteronomy 21:1-2
            • Testimony is taken when innocent blood is spilled Deuteronomy 21:7
            • Requested for atonement when innocent blood is spilled Deuteronomy 21:8
            • Responsibility in passing down the works of God to their children Deuteronomy 32:7
            • Witnesses in transactions Ruth 4:9
            • Israel sinned when the generation of elders that had seen the works of God died Judges 2:7-10
          • In the empire:
            • Associated with the Senate (Senatus => Senex => elder or council of elders) 7
              • In control of religion and governance of the provinces
            • Considered honourable and respectable men
            • Often powerful men, held positions of power and authority
          • In the church:
            • Very likely based on the Old Testament model
  • Qualifications of elders:
    • Above reproach v.6, 7
    • Husband of one wife v.6
    • Children: v.6
      • Are believers v.6
      • Not open to the charge of debauchery or insubordination v.6
        • Debauchery: Corruption of fidelity or overindulgence in lust and the like (Dictionary definition)
        • Insubordinate: Act of refusal to submit or obey to lawful authority (Dictionary definition)
    • Q: Why is a man’s relationship with and the status of his family important as a potential overseer?
      • Overseers must be above reproach v.7
      • They are God’s stewards v.7
      • They have led their families in a godly manner, so God now puts them in the position of elder in the broader family of God Matthew 25:14-30
    • Not be arrogant, drunkard, quick-tempered, violent, greedy for gain v.7
      • Q: Why does Paul insert this in the middle of describing positive characteristics for overseers?
        • Probably to serve as a focal point to bring attention to it
        • Serves as the connecting factor between how an elder leads his family and how he treats others
    • Hospitable v.8
    • Lover of good v.8
    • Self-controlled v.8
    • Upright v.8
    • Holy v.8
      • Q: Is there a difference between being holy and being upright?
        • Upright is closely tied in with being right
          • Related to being righteous
        • Holiness is associated with being set apart for God
          • Associated with purity and dedication to God
        • Because we are upright (righteous) before God, we are being made holy
          • God is holy and is always righteous
          • When we become righteous, we become more and more holy as God purifies us from within 8
        • Probably not the best question in the world, as definitions must come from the Bible
    • Disciplined v.8
    • Hold firm to the trustworthy word as taught v.8
      • Give instruction in sound doctrine v.8
      • Rebuke those who contradict it v.8
    • Why: Overseers are God’s stewards v.7
      • Q: What are overseers stewarding?
        • They are to look after the church
    • Q: How does each one of these qualities relate to being God’s stewards?

This is important because there are people within the church of Crete who are teaching false doctrine. Their teachings are becoming a stumbling block to entire families. Even their own say that they are not good people and as such, they should be rebuked. Such people say they know God but their works say otherwise. v.10-16

  • Contrasting to qualifications for elders v.10-16
    • Q: Why does Paul feel the need to contrast the two groups of people?
  • Characteristics of the people who Paul is denouncing
    • Insubordinate v.10
    • Empty talkers and deceivers v.10
      • Particularly those in the circumcision party v.10
        • Q: What is the circumcision party (ie. Who are they? What do they teach?)?
  • What must happen to such people:
    • Must be silenced v.11
    • Why:
      • Upsetting whole families v.11
      • Teaching for shameful gain what they should not be teaching v.11
  • Their own prophets talk negatively about themselves v.12
    • Liars, evil beasts, lazy gluttons v.12
    • Paul affirms this v.13
  • Such people must be rebuked sharply v.13
    • Why:
      • That they may be sound in faith v.13
        • Q: What does it look like for someone to be sound in faith?
      • Not devote themselves to Jewish myths v.14
        • Q: What sort of myths are the Jewish people promoting?
      • Not devote themselves to the commands of people who turn away from the truth v.14
  • To the pure, all things are pure v.15
    • Q: What is meant by “all things”?
  • To the defiled and unbelievers, nothing is pure v.15
    • Minds and consciences are defiled v.15
    • Profess to know God v.16
    • Deny God by their works v.16
    • Detestable, disobedient, unfit for any good work v.16
  • Q: Are the people Paul addressing here believers, unbelievers, or both?
