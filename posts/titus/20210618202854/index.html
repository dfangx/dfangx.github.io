Titus 3

Posted at — Jun 18, 2021

The people in Crete are reminded in how they are supposed to live towards authorities, other believers, and towards the general population. This is in contrast with their former selves, until they were saved by the goodness and loving kindness of God. Their salvation is solely based on the work of Christ, so that they may also be heirs with Christ. Such things should be insisted on, while pointless talk and quarrels are to be avoided. If someone does the latter, then they are self-condemned. v.1-11

Paul ends the letter with final instructions about the people he is sending to Crete, the people being sent from Crete, and to continue to do good. He and the people with him send their greetings. v.12-15
